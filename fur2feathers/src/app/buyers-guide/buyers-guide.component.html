Pet insurance is a way to prepare for expected and unexpected veterinary costs. It is a form of property insurance and works similarly to an auto or home insurance policy. Pet insurance is most often set up so that you pay your veterinarian up-front and are reimbursed a percentage of the bill after you have met your annual deductible.

Veterinary care is expensive. In 2018 Americans spent $72 billion dollars on their pets, $18.11 billion specifically on veterinary care. This amount is increasing at approximately 6.1% per year. While every pet parent would love to say that money is no object when it comes to the health of their furry, scaley, or feathered family member, it is the sad truth that many Americans simply cannot afford extended care for pet illness or the unexpected expenses of emergency care. Sometimes it means saying no to needed diagnostic tests or in extreme cases, losing a beloved family member due to financial constraints. Pet insurance is a way to prepare for the unexpected.

Your monthly payments are defined by many factors, including (but not limited to):
  • The regulations of the state you live in
  • The species of your pet
  • The age of your pet
  • The average cost of veterinary care in your area
  • The deductible and yearly maximum benefits you choose
